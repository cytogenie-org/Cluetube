Protocol designer

  • Type protocol and study names by which you will retrieve this protocol.
  • Type or use the arrows to enter your intended execution date. Change the date whenever you want.
  • Type or use the arrows to enter your target species.
  • Add any comments or notes you want to save with your protocol.
  • Enter web page addresses (URLs) and comments in Add web resources.Add web resources.
  • Specify where on your computer to save files for this protocol in Associated files and Associated files alias.
  • Specify the web address for your data archive in Evertrieve or other data URL.
Save time by reusing Routine protocols or My protocols.(Drag & drop the links on your web browser)

Routine protocols 

  • Reuse the same protocol with the same stain set and instrument setting. Change only your subjects and samples.
  • To designate any protocol as routine, go to the bottom of the To do list and click Add this protocol to routine protocol list.
  • To view your list, go to the menu bar and click Routine protocols. Click the one you want to reuse. CytoGenie will open it and return you to Protocol designer.

My Protocols

  • Find and reuse any protocol that you have created or inherited from your laboratory and FACS facility. If you have not previously archived the protocol, you can edit it. Or you can copy it and use it as a template for a new protocol.
  • To see your protocol list, go to the menu bar and click My protocols.
  • Click the one you want to use. CytoGenie will open it and return you to Protocol designer.

Specify controls

  • Click to check or uncheck each of the controls that CytoGenie AutoColor offers – compensation controls, unstained controls, calibration controls, FMO controls.
  • Click to check or uncheck Ignore reagent availability and Allow multiple lots per stain set.
  • If you choose to use compensation controls, a table will display to the right of the screen. Click to check or uncheck whether to use unstained for DiVa.
  • Click to check or uncheck whether to use capture beads for each dye/color.
  • If you choose not to use the capture beads, a window will display a table of your subjects and tissues. Click to select the row you want to use. Click Pick.
(Drag & drop the above links on your web browser)

Show plate/rack layout

  • To change your layout groups, double-click the blue area at the left.
  • A window will appear. Click to select or unselect the columns to use in grouping wells and tubes or plates and racks.
  • Click the downward arrows at the top of the screen to see your layout options. Click to select the ones you want.
  • If you want to change the number of rows and columns, double-click the blank and type a new value.

Print experiment

  • Go to the menu bar and click Documents.
  • A menu will drop down. Click Print experiment.
  • A list of your pipetting guide sections will display. Click to uncheck any that you do not want to print at this time. You can always come back to print or reprint any of the sections.
  • To change the sequence of sections, click to select any row. Click the up or down arrow to reposition your selection. Repeat until you have the order you want.
  • Click Done.
  • The Pipetting Guide will open in pdf format. Click to view any entry in the Table of Contents.
  • Click the adobe printer icon to print a copy.

ClueTube Index











Export to instrument

  • Click Documents
  • A menu will appear. Click Export to instrument.
  • If you are exporting to DiVa, a window will ask which specimen group to use. Click to check either Subject, Stain set, Tissue or Rack/Plate Row.
  • Click Ok.
  • A window will inform you that CytoGenie has printed the protocol to your file system. If you want to review the file before exporting to the server, click Cancel. You will find the file on your computer at the address you entered on the protocol overview screen in Associated files.
  • To continue exporting, click Start.

Set or change your preferences

CytoGenie keeps track of how you arrange your user interface and saves these in your preferences. To open the preferences screen:

  • Go to the bottom of the To do list and click Review/Edit preferences.
  • Or click Edit on the program menu. A sub-menu will display. Click Preferences.

Change any preference you want. You can always click Factory settings to restore the original preferences. Some preferences will not go into effect until you relaunch CytoGenie.
